Flexibility from industrial demand-side management in net-zero sector-coupled national energy systems
energy systems
industry
optimization

Using a generic, process-agnostic model, we determine the value of industrial demand-side management within larger energy systems. We show that demand-side management can reduce total energy system costs by up to 4.4% or 20% of industry-related energy costs. However, the value of demand-side management depends on the system’s flexibility alternatives.

Patricia Mayer, Mario Heer, David Yang Shu, Nik Zielonka, Ludger Leenders, Florian Joseph Baader, André Bardow

Overcoming the central planner approach – Bilevel optimization of the European energy transition
energy systems
LCA
optimization

We model distributed decision making in the transition to net-zero energy systems via bilevel optimization. By comparing the results of the transition to a model following the central planner approach, we find that neglecting stakeholder representation in energy system models can result in miscalculations of the need for capacity expansion.

David Yang Shu, Christiane Reinert, Jacob Mannhardt, Ludger Leenders, Jannik Lüthje, Alexander Mitsos, André Bardow
